Graduation Week: A Video Diary

About the Film, Dandellions & Wishes

Graduation Week: A Video Diary captures two moments side by side. One is vibrant and full of life: graduation week, with its flashing cameras, shouted names, embraces, tears, and laughter. It’s the energy of something ending and something else beginning. The other moment comes a week later. The same places, now still. Palm trees unmoving, the buildings quiet, the air calm. The camera returns to where everything happened, and just waits. By layering these two states — the commotion and the calm — the film becomes a way to hold onto what’s already slipping past. It doesn’t explain, it simply notices. It says: we were here, and it meant something. The wish section follows that same feeling. It’s here for anything you want to release: a memory, a thank you, a hope for what’s next. If you feel like sharing, there’s a form below where you can write a wish. It won’t be shown, just quietly received. Like blowing on a dandelion: you don’t need to say it out loud. You just send it off and let the wind take it from there.
